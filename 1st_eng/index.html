1. Executive Summary

Strategic Opportunity: The Philippine construction market is experiencing an explosive surge in demand for cost management and forecasting solutions like Costify, driven by the convergence of the government's 'Build Better More' program (valued at USD 176.7 billion) and rapid digital transformation (82% of firms participating). The high cost and low localization of existing solutions (e.g., Procore) present a compelling entry opportunity for Costify.

This report presents a concrete strategy for Costify to gain initial traction in the Philippine market and establish a foothold effectively without costly enterprise marketing. The key components are as follows:

  • Market Size: A high-growth market expanding from USD 43.44 billion in 2025 to USD 63.77 billion by 2031 (CAGR 6.61%).
  • Core Target: Innovation-oriented Tier 1 contractors with government infrastructure projects (e.g., Megawide, EEI) and mid-tier general contractors under significant cost pressure.
  • Differentiation Strategy: Positioning around 'cost-effective AI forecasting' and 'flexible integration with existing systems' to differentiate from Procore's high-cost, closed ecosystem.
  • Execution Plan: Focus on securing references through key local partnerships (e.g., Megawide) and building thought leadership via digital channels.

2.2 Government Infrastructure Investment: Build Better More (BBM)

The Marcos Jr. administration's 'Build Better More' program represents the single largest opportunity factor for Costify. Government-led projects require transparent cost management and schedule compliance, which directly aligns with Costify's core value proposition.

  • Project Scale: 207 Infrastructure Flagship Projects (IFPs) approved.
  • Total Investment: Approximately USD 176.7 billion (PHP 9.6 trillion).
  • Focus Areas: Primarily large-scale civil works including roads, bridges, mass transit, and water resource management — matching Costify's strength in the civil engineering domain.

2.3 Digital Transformation Status

The Philippine construction industry is actively embracing digital tools, moving away from legacy manual processes. According to Procore's survey, 82% of Philippine construction firms have already initiated digital transformation measures. However, many companies still face data silos and fragmented legacy systems, indicating strong latent demand for Costify's 'ease of integration' capability.

3.2 Costify's Competitive Advantage

  • Cost Efficiency: Provides a viable alternative for mid-tier contractors (Tier 2–3) who find adopting high-cost solutions like Procore financially prohibitive.
  • AI-Powered Forecasting: Goes beyond simple recording to proactively warn about 'upcoming cost overrun risks' based on historical data.
  • Flexible Integration: Connects with existing ERP/accounting systems rather than requiring a "rip-and-replace" approach, resolving data silo issues.

4. Target Customer & Pain Points

4.1 Ideal Customer Profile

  • Company Type: Mid-tier civil and building general contractors with annual revenue of USD 50M–500M.
  • Primary Projects: Government-issued infrastructure (roads, bridges) or medium-to-large commercial buildings.
  • Decision Makers (Budget Owners): CFO, Project Director, Commercial Manager.
  • End Users: Project Managers (PM), Site Superintendents, Quantity Surveyors.

4.2 Key Pain Points

  1. Reactive Cost Management: Cost overruns are only identified after they occur, resulting in failed margin protection.
  2. Data Fragmentation: Discrepancies between field data (paper/Excel) and headquarters ERP data cause decision-making delays.
  3. Skilled Labor Shortage: A shortage of experienced PMs in the Philippines makes system-based risk management critically necessary.
  4. Regulatory Compliance Pressure: Increasing transparency requirements for government projects demand accurate data reporting.

Avoid high-cost marketing and focus on trust-based B2B channels.

5.2 Partnership Target Companies

The following three companies are selected as top-priority targets due to their strong market influence and proactive stance on digital transformation in the Philippine market.

  • Megawide Construction Corp: The most innovative EPC firm in the Philippines, actively adopting new technologies such as precast construction. Best aligned with Costify's 'AI innovation' positioning.
  • EEI Corporation: A major player in large-scale infrastructure and industrial plant construction. The complexity of their project management creates strong appeal for Costify's 'risk forecasting' capabilities.
  • DMCI Holdings: A diversified housing and infrastructure conglomerate. Target their need for integrated dashboards to manage diverse project portfolios.

5.3 Pricing Strategy

Considering the price sensitivity of the Philippine market, a 'value-based tiered pricing model' is proposed.

  • Pilot Plan (Entry-Level): Single-project scope with core features (cost tracking, basic reporting). Low barrier to entry.
  • Growth Plan (Primary): Up to 5 projects, includes AI forecasting and risk alerts. Unlimited users (emphasizing collaboration).
  • Enterprise Plan: Unlimited projects, API integration support, dedicated CS manager assigned.
