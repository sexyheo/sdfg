02 — Market Analysis

Asymmetric Digitalization

Transactions Are Digital

E-commerce adoption at 41.3%, mobile work at 62.9%, and government digital interaction at 58.5%. The Philippines' digital payment share has reached 57%.

The government's eGov PH app expansion, rising EGDI scores, and BIR EIS (Electronic Invoicing System) mandatory rollout are accelerating administrative digitalization.

But Processes Are Analog

Only 8.5% of construction workers regularly use computers — the lowest across all industries (industry average: 41.9%). BIM adoption stands at roughly one-third, with similar proportions unaware of the concept entirely.

Digital Maturity Snapshot

  • 82% of 259 surveyed firms have taken some form of digital transformation action
  • 26% are digital-first; 56% are "progressing well" — ~80% at mid-level or above
  • 65% of decision-makers increased digital investment in the past 24 months
  • IoT (47%), drones (43%), telematics (42%), big data (41%) — all above APAC averages
  • Technology adoption in Philippine construction grew 400% over 3 years (ADB)
  • 99% of executives say efficient data management is a core competitive advantage

Key Insight: "Transactions are digital, but processes are analog." The gap between government platforms and on-site data management is Costify's entry point.

Procore vs Costify: Differentiation

Procore — "The Ecosystem Giant"

  • Full-lifecycle platform: PM, safety, invoices, ERP integration, real-time collaboration
  • Official MOU with Philippine Constructors Association (PCA) — trained 150+ professionals
  • Makati Development Corporation deployment as local reference case
  • 4.4-4.7/5 satisfaction, but steep learning curve and high cost cited on Reddit & community forums
  • Annual cost $100,000+ — prohibitive for mid-sized Philippine firms

Procore's Weakness: "Hilariously bad UI" for subcontractors. Field workers and small subs resist adoption — data gaps follow.

Costify — "The Precision Blade"

  • Specialized in financial anomaly detection and workforce productivity visualization
  • AI-driven risk prediction: combines live project data with historical patterns
  • Cloud-native with real-time cost tracking during execution phase
  • Institutional Knowledge Base: captures retiring engineers' expertise digitally
  • Affordable pricing accessible to mid-market contractors

Positioning: "Procore is for managing. Costify is for controlling." Focus on where money leaks — not project documentation.

04 — Pain Points

Four Pillars of Construction Pain

$

1. Financial & Risk Management Failure

90% of large projects exceed budget and schedule. Average overrun is 65%. Construction margins hover around 5% — any overrun threatens survival. Current tools deliver "days-old data" — reactive, not predictive. Users discover budget blowouts after it's too late.

K

2. Knowledge & Workforce Crisis

25% skilled workforce shortage projected by 2026. Retiring experts take decades of institutional knowledge with them ("Brain Drain"). 70% of project failures trace back to knowledge-sharing failures. 60% of Australian construction PMs have degrees but under 5 years of field experience.

T

3. Tool Limitations — Cost & Complexity

Procore costs $100K+/year — unaffordable for mid-sized firms. "Hilariously bad UI" drives field rejection. Subcontractors "who struggle with email" cannot adopt Procore. Organizations cling to error-prone Excel out of change aversion — fragmented, no real-time sharing, no version control.

D

4. Operational Inefficiency & Data Silos

Accounting, scheduling, procurement each use different tools — data scattered. 20+ years of historical project data sits untapped ("mountains of untapped data"). Only 15% of firms use digital tools effectively. In the Philippines, non-cloud tools worsen field accessibility and data fragmentation.
